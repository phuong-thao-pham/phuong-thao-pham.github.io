Resources

Thao P. Pham · University of Wisconsin–Madison

A curated collection of resources, blogs, and guides on economics, education policy, advanced statistics, and academic workflows that I find highly valuable.

Academic Life & Writing

  • Chris Blattman's PhD Advice – General advice on navigating grad school, research, and academia (a favorite of mine since applying for my PhD).
  • Matt Might's Articles – Excellent general tips on productivity, academic writing, and effective computation.
  • The Thesis Whisperer – Highly recommended strategies and insights for writing, teaching, and academic learning.
  • Raúl Pacheco-Vega's Blog – In-depth, practical guides on literature reviews, reading strategies, and research management.

AI, Machine Learning & Workflow

  • Dario Sansone: ML & AI for Economists – An exhaustive compilation of machine learning and artificial intelligence resources tailored for econometric applications.
  • Pedro H. C. Sant'Anna: Claude Code Workflow Guide – A deep dive into integrating terminal-based AI tools into computational social science workflows.

Education Policy & Finance

  • School Finance 101 – Expert analysis and deep dives into the economics and mechanics of school funding.
  • Dominique Baker – Great resources on higher education policy, academia, and navigating grant writing.
  • Kelchen on Education – Regular data-driven insights and commentary on higher education policy and finance by Robert Kelchen.
  • Rick Hess Straight Up – Straight-talking analysis of education policy, politics, and school reform.
  • The Education Policy Hub Substack – Timely updates, policy briefs, and research roundups on current education policy.
  • James Pustejovsky's Blog – Excellent posts focusing on meta-analysis methodology, single-case designs, and applied statistics in education research.
  • Roxanne Rivas' Resources – A helpful compilation of tools and datasets geared toward education research.

Statistics & Econometrics

  • Frank Harrell's Blog – Essential reading on biostatistics, clinical trial design, and robust predictive modeling.
  • Simply Statistics – Practical discussion of data science, statistical ethics, and the evolving role of biostatistics.
  • Clément de Chaisemartin – Essential working papers and software packages covering cutting-edge developments in Difference-in-Differences (DiD) and causal inference.
  • Solomon Kurz – A fantastic, code-heavy resource focused on Stan, Bayesian data analysis, and statistical modeling.

Quantitative Economics & Data Science

  • Causal Inference: The Mixtape (Scott Cunningham) – Invaluable insights, interviews, and pedagogical content focused on modern causal inference.
  • Grant McDermott – Highly practical guides, tutorials, and notes combining data science, computing, and environmental/applied economics.

Education News & Policy Commentary

  • Education Week – Premier news source covering K-12 education policy, practice, and national trends.
  • Brown Center Chalkboard (Brookings Institution) – Regularly updated, data-driven research and commentary on pressing challenges in education policy.
